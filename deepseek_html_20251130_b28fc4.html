Welcome Chocolate Lovers!

Chocolate is one of the most popular food types and flavors in the world, and many foodstuffs involving chocolate exist, particularly desserts, including cakes, pudding, mousse, chocolate brownies, and chocolate chip cookies.

Many candies are filled with or coated with sweetened chocolate, and bars of solid chocolate and candy bars coated in chocolate are eaten as snacks. Gifts of chocolate molded into different shapes have become traditional on certain holidays: chocolate bunnies and eggs are popular on Easter, chocolate coins on Hanukkah, Santa Claus and other holiday symbols on Christmas, and hearts on Valentine's Day.

Why We Love Chocolate

  • Rich in antioxidants that help fight free radicals
  • May improve heart health by lowering blood pressure
  • Can boost mood and reduce stress through serotonin release
  • Contains important minerals like iron, magnesium, and copper
  • May improve brain function and cognitive performance

Chocolate Fun Facts

Did you know that it takes approximately 400 cocoa beans to make one pound of chocolate? Each cacao tree produces approximately 2,500 beans. The ancient Maya people believed that chocolate was discovered by the gods and was considered more valuable than gold!

"All you need is love. But a little chocolate now and then doesn't hurt." - Charles M. Schulz

Chocolate Consumption Worldwide

Switzerland leads the world in chocolate consumption with the average Swiss person eating about 19.8 pounds of chocolate annually. The United States ranks 15th with the average American consuming about 9.5 pounds per year.
