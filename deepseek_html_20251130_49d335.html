The Ancient Origins

The history of chocolate began in Mesoamerica. Fermented beverages made from chocolate date back to 450 BC. The Mexica believed that cacao seeds were the gift of Quetzalcoatl, the god of wisdom, and the seeds once had so much value that they were used as a form of currency.

Key Historical Moments

  1. 1500 BC - 400 BC: The Olmecs are believed to be the first to grow cocoa beans as a domestic crop.
  2. 250 AD - 900 AD: The Maya celebrate chocolate as the drink of the gods.
  3. 1200 AD - 1521 AD: The Aztecs use cocoa beans as currency and continue the tradition of chocolate drinks.
  4. 1502: Christopher Columbus encounters cocoa beans during his fourth voyage to the Americas.
  5. 1528: Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés brings chocolate to Europe.
  6. 1657: The first chocolate house opens in London.
  7. 1765: Chocolate is introduced to the United States.
  8. 1847: Joseph Fry creates the first modern chocolate bar.
  9. 1875: Daniel Peter invents milk chocolate.
  10. 1900: Milton S. Hershey introduces the Hershey chocolate bar.

Ancient Chocolate Preparation

Originally prepared only as a drink, chocolate was served as a bitter liquid, mixed with spices or corn puree. It was believed to be an aphrodisiac and to give the drinker strength. Today, such drinks are also known as "Chilate" and are made by locals in the South of Mexico.
